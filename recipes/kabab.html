Shami Kabab Recipe

Description

Shami kabab is a popular Pakistani and Indian dish made from minced or ground meat, typically beef or lamb, combined with spices and formed into patties. The patties are then fried or grilled and served with chutney or raita.

Ingredients

  • 500g minced beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch piece ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 cup chana dal, soaked for 1 hour
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Salt, to taste

Steps

  1. In a pan, add the minced meat, chopped onion, minced garlic, grated ginger, cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, chana dal, and salt. Mix well.
  2. Add enough water to cover the mixture, cover the pan, and cook over low heat until the meat is tender and the water has evaporated, about 45 minutes to an hour.
  3. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool. Once cooled, grind the mixture to a fine paste in a food processor or grinder.
  4. Shape the mixture into small patties or kababs and dip each in the beaten egg.
  5. Heat oil in a frying pan and fry the patties over medium heat until golden brown on both sides.
  6. Remove the patties from the pan and serve hot with chutney or raita.
